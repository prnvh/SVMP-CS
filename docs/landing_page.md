# SVMP Public Landing Page

## Purpose

The public landing page is the marketing and trust-building site for SVMP. It should live at `svmpsystems.com`.

This is separate from the paid customer portal at `app.svmpsystems.com`.

```text
svmpsystems.com      -> public landing and sales site
app.svmpsystems.com  -> private customer dashboard
api.svmpsystems.com  -> FastAPI API and webhooks
```

## Goal

The landing page should explain SVMP clearly enough that a business owner understands:

- what SVMP does
- who it is for
- what problem it solves
- why it is safer than a generic chatbot
- what they get after paying
- how to request access or book a demo

The page should not feel like a generic AI SaaS site. It should feel like a serious AI support system for businesses that care about control, safety, and measurable savings.

## Reference Inspiration

Use Anthropic as directional inspiration, not as a template to copy.

Current Anthropic and Claude pages have a few useful patterns:

- lead with a serious mission, not feature noise
- make safety and trust central from the first screen
- use calm editorial pacing
- use short, confident copy
- let whitespace and typography do more of the work
- treat product updates, research, and trust materials as proof
- avoid exaggerated AI hype

For SVMP, translate that into:

```text
controlled AI support
approved knowledge
clear escalation rules
auditable decisions
measurable support savings
```

Do not copy Anthropic's wording, layout, brand identity, or palette directly. SVMP should borrow the restraint and credibility, then make it specific to customer support automation.

## Visual Direction

The landing page should feel calm, precise, and operational.

Recommended direction:

- large editorial headline
- simple top navigation
- off-white or clean light background with strong contrast
- restrained accent color used sparingly
- product dashboard imagery as the main visual proof
- real interface previews instead of abstract AI decoration
- clear section dividers with generous spacing
- dense footer with product, company, and trust links

Avoid:

- loud gradient AI visuals
- bokeh blobs or decorative orbs
- fake futuristic dashboards
- overuse of cards
- generic robot imagery
- dominant beige or sand palette, even though Anthropic often leans warm and editorial
- claims like "fully autonomous" or "replace your whole support team"

The page should look like a company that handles real customer conversations, not like a launch-week AI toy.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- small and mid-sized businesses handling repetitive customer support
- ecommerce, D2C, services, clinics, salons, local businesses, and support-heavy brands
- founders or operators who use WhatsApp heavily

Secondary audience:

- teams already using human support agents
- businesses that want AI automation but are worried about hallucinations
- businesses that need auditability and escalation control

## Core Message

Recommended headline direction:

```text
AI customer support that knows when to answer and when to escalate.
```

Supporting message:

```text
SVMP connects to WhatsApp, uses your approved knowledge base, escalates when confidence is low, and gives you a dashboard to inspect every answer.
```

Alternative headline options:

```text
AI support governed by your knowledge, your voice, and your rules.
```

```text
WhatsApp support automation with a built-in trust layer.
```

```text
Let AI answer the safe questions. Escalate the rest.
```

## Positioning

SVMP is not just a chatbot.

It is:

- a WhatsApp support automation system
- a tenant-aware customer service backend
- a governed answer engine
- a knowledge-base controlled assistant
- an audit layer for AI support decisions

Avoid positioning it as:

- a generic AI bot
- a public ChatGPT wrapper
- a replacement for all support staff
- a fully self-serve SaaS marketplace in v1

## Page Sections

### Hero

Purpose: say what SVMP does immediately.

Needs:

- clear headline
- short supporting line
- primary CTA: `Book a demo`
- secondary CTA: `View dashboard preview` or `See how it works`
- visual direction: product/dashboard screenshot or immersive support workflow visual

Do not make the hero vague. The user should understand that SVMP handles customer support through controlled AI automation.

Anthropic-inspired adaptation:

- lead with a mission-style statement, but make it practical
- keep the hero copy short
- use one strong product visual instead of many floating feature cards
- make safety visible above the fold through words like `approved knowledge`, `confidence`, `escalation`, and `audit trail`

Possible hero copy:

```text
AI customer support that knows when to answer and when to escalate.
```

```text
SVMP helps businesses automate WhatsApp support using approved knowledge, brand voice controls, confidence thresholds, and a dashboard for every answer.
```

### Problem

Purpose: describe the pain.

Possible points:

- customers ask the same questions every day
- WhatsApp conversations arrive in fragments
- human teams lose time on repetitive replies
- generic AI can hallucinate or overpromise
- owners need visibility into what AI said and why

### How SVMP Works

Purpose: explain the system simply.

Suggested steps:

1. Connect WhatsApp.
2. Add approved knowledge.
3. Set brand voice and escalation rules.
4. SVMP answers safe questions.
5. SVMP escalates uncertain questions.
6. You inspect results in the dashboard.

### Product Pillars

Purpose: show what makes SVMP different.

Pillars:

- Knowledge-controlled answers
- Brand voice control
- Confidence-based escalation
- Governance logs
- Metrics and support savings
- Tenant-safe dashboard

Anthropic-inspired section style:

- use fewer pillars with stronger explanations
- keep the titles plain
- show proof beside each pillar when possible

Recommended pillars:

```text
Controlled answers
SVMP answers from the knowledge you approve.

Human fallback
Low-confidence questions go to escalation instead of guesswork.

Inspectable decisions
Every answer includes context, confidence, source, and reason.

Measurable savings
Track deflection, human hours saved, resolution time, and safety.
```

### Dashboard Preview

Purpose: connect the public site to the paid-client portal.

Show or describe:

- deflection rate
- human hours saved
- safety score
- sessions
- knowledge base
- brand voice
- governance logs

This section should make the dashboard feel like the product customers get after paying.

This should be one of the most important sections. The public site should not only say "trust layer"; it should show that customers get a real operating console.

Recommended visual:

- one large dashboard screenshot or realistic product mock
- smaller supporting rows for sessions, knowledge base, and governance
- no fake-connected integrations
- no made-up performance numbers unless marked as sample data

### Use Cases

Purpose: make the product concrete.

Examples:

- answer product questions
- explain pricing and offers
- handle shipping and return FAQs
- collect unclear cases for human follow-up
- keep support answers consistent across agents
- identify missing KB entries from real customer questions

### Trust And Safety

Purpose: reduce fear around AI support.

Key points:

- SVMP answers from approved knowledge
- low-confidence questions escalate
- every answer leaves a governance trail
- businesses control brand voice
- settings include confidence thresholds
- unsafe or unsupported answers can be blocked

Anthropic-inspired adaptation:

Make this section central, not buried. SVMP's strongest landing-page idea is that automation is useful because it is governed.

Suggested section headline:

```text
Built for support teams that need control, not surprises.
```

Suggested points:

```text
Answers are grounded in approved KB entries.
Confidence thresholds decide when automation is allowed.
Escalations are logged with reasons.
Owners can inspect what SVMP said and why.
```

### Integrations

Purpose: show current and future channels honestly.

MVP:

- WhatsApp

Later:

- Slack
- Shopify
- Zendesk

Do not imply future integrations are live before they are.

### Call To Action

Purpose: convert interest into a conversation.

Recommended CTA:

```text
Book a demo
```

Secondary CTA:

```text
Request access
```

Since the first version is a private paid-client portal, the CTA should not overpromise instant public self-serve signup.

## Navigation

Simple top nav:

- How it works
- Dashboard
- Safety
- Integrations
- Pricing or Demo
- Login

`Login` should send users to `app.svmpsystems.com/login`.

## Pricing Direction

For v1, pricing can be demo-led instead of fully public self-serve.

Options:

- show `Book a demo` instead of public pricing
- show simple starting price if the offer is finalized
- avoid complex pricing tables until packaging is stable

## Copy Rules

Use plain language.

Good:

```text
SVMP answers repeat customer questions on WhatsApp using your approved knowledge base.
```

Avoid:

```text
Revolutionize your support with next-generation autonomous AI agents.
```

The tone should be confident, concrete, and calm.

Anthropic-style restraint for SVMP:

- use short sentences
- prefer nouns like `knowledge`, `evidence`, `confidence`, `audit`, and `control`
- avoid breathless adjectives
- explain value with operational examples
- talk about safety as a product behavior, not a slogan

Good:

```text
When SVMP is not confident, it escalates instead of guessing.
```

Avoid:

```text
Our revolutionary safety-first framework unlocks limitless autonomous support.
```

## MVP Landing Page Scope

First version should include:

- hero
- problem
- how it works
- product pillars
- dashboard preview
- trust and safety
- WhatsApp integration
- CTA
- login link

Skip for v1:

- blog
- resource center
- public docs
- complex pricing calculator
- marketplace pages
- deep case studies unless real data exists

## Relationship To Customer Portal

The landing page sells and explains SVMP.

The customer portal lets paid businesses operate SVMP.

```text
Landing page: "Why should I use SVMP?"
Customer portal: "How is my SVMP system performing and configured?"
```

These should be planned separately even if they eventually share visual design.

## Build Notes

When we build the landing page, keep it separate from the dashboard app logic even if both live in the future `web/` project.

Recommended first implementation:

```text
web/app/page.tsx                 # public landing page
web/app/login/page.tsx           # dashboard login
web/app/dashboard/page.tsx       # private portal
web/components/landing/*         # landing-only sections
web/components/dashboard/*       # portal-only components
```

The landing page can use screenshots, product mockups, and public CTAs. It should not call private dashboard APIs.
